    NSBE

    The National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) stands as a beacon of academic and professional development, offering crucial support and community for black students in engineering and STEM fields to thrive during and after their college years. As one of the nation's largest student-run organizations, NSBE extends its reach to empower Black students globally, providing a wealth of programming, networking avenues, community service projects, and career planning resources.

    HU Robotics club

    HURO - Howard University Robotics Organization. The biggest dilemma amongst college students is that we take in so much information in a short amount of time, and as a result we lose a lot of that information because there's no place to apply it. That's why the goal of HURO is to create an environment in which engineering students can use the information they learn in classes and apply it to hands-on projects. By doing so, we hope to increase the equity and skill of black engineers across the Howard University Campus. Join us in being the HUROs of Howard!

    HU Skate Club

    The Howard University Skateboarding Club was founded by Monyell Sessoms and Kameren Haliday in Fall of 2022. Its purpose is to promote skateboarding and skate culture to black and brown students while providing a safe and inclusive experience for all its members.
